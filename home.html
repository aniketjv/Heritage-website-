A Story Carved in Stone

Maharashtra has a long and glorious history that dates back to ancient times. Powerful dynasties like the Satavahanas and Rashtrakutas ruled the region and contributed to trade, art, and architecture, creating famous monuments such as the Ajanta and Ellora Caves. These rulers helped shape Maharashtra’s cultural and religious heritage.

The most remarkable period came in the 17th century under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, who founded the Maratha Empire and built strong forts and administration while resisting Mughal rule. Later, the Marathas became a dominant power in India before the British took control. After India’s independence, Maharashtra was officially formed as a separate state on 1 May 1960.
